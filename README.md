## 1. 2025 Annual Income Tax (ITR) Brackets

The first **₱250,000** of annual taxable income is **tax-exempt**. The table below shows the progressive tax schedule for Compensation Income Earners and Self-Employed Individuals/Professionals who opt for the Graduated Tax Rate.

| Annual Taxable Income (PHP) | Tax Due (Base Amount) | Tax Rate on Excess |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Up to ₱250,000** | ₱0 | **EXEMPT** |
| **Over ₱250,000 but not over ₱400,000** | ₱0 | **15%** of the excess over ₱250,000 |
| **Over ₱400,000 but not over ₱800,000** | ₱22,500 | **20%** of the excess over ₱400,000 |
| **Over ₱800,000 but not over ₱2,000,000** | ₱102,500 | **25%** of the excess over ₱800,000 |
| **Over ₱2,000,000 but not over ₱8,000,000** | ₱402,500 | **30%** of the excess over ₱2,000,000 |
| **Over ₱8,000,000** | ₱2,202,500 | **35%** of the excess over ₱8,000,000 |

### Self-Employed and Professionals Note:
Self-employed individuals and professionals whose gross sales/receipts **do not exceed ₱3,000,000** (the VAT threshold) may opt for a simplified **8% tax rate** on gross sales/receipts **in excess of ₱250,000**, in lieu of the graduated tax and percentage tax.

## 2. Non-Taxable Compensation and Benefits

Certain employee benefits are exempt from income tax up to a specific limit. Your tax calculator must account for these exclusions to arrive at the correct **Taxable Income**.

### A. 13th Month Pay and Other Benefits Ceiling

The total amount of **13th Month Pay** (mandatory benefit) and **Other Benefits** (e.g., Christmas Bonus, Performance Bonuses, 14th Month Pay, and the *excess* of De Minimis benefits) is exempt from tax up to a total of:

* **Tax Exemption Ceiling:** **₱90,000** per employee per year.

***Note:*** *Any amount exceeding the ₱90,000 limit is added to the employee's regular compensation and becomes subject to the graduated income tax rates.*

### B. Tax-Exempt De Minimis Benefits

**De Minimis Benefits** are small fringe benefits given by employers that are exempt from tax *without* being included in the ₱90,000 ceiling. These are:

| Benefit Type | Tax-Exempt Limit (Per Employee) |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Rice Subsidy** | Up to **₱2,000** per month |
| **Uniform/Clothing Allowance** | Up to **₱7,000** per annum (Increased from ₱6,000 by RR No. 004-2025) |
| **Medical Cash Allowance to Dependents** | Up to **₱1,500** per semester or **₱250** per month |
| **Laundry Allowance** | Up to **₱300** per month |
| **Actual Medical Assistance (Annual Check-up, etc.)** | Up to **₱10,000** per annum |
| **Monetized Unused Vacation Leave** | Up to **10 days** per annum |
| **Employee Achievement Awards (Tangible Property)** | Up to **₱10,000** per annum |
| **Gifts (Christmas/Anniversary)** | Up to **₱5,000** per annum |
| **Daily Meal Allowance (Overtime/Night Shift)** | Up to **25%** of the basic minimum wage (on a per region basis) |
| **CBA & Productivity Incentive Schemes** | Up to **₱10,000** per annum (Total combined) |

## 3. Mandatory Government Contributions

While not part of the income tax computation, mandatory contributions are typically **excluded from taxable income** for employees. These amounts should be deducted from the Gross Income before applying the tax brackets.

* **SSS** (Social Security System)
* **PhilHealth** (Philippine Health Insurance Corporation)
* **Pag-IBIG/HDMF** (Home Development Mutual Fund)
